Getting Started — Do This First

  1. 1
    Add your inventory

    Go to Manage → Inventory. Add every product (box) you carry. Enter the product name, cost per box, category (Baseball, Basketball, Football, etc.), and your starting stock quantity.

    Stock decrements automatically each time a break is submitted. You can restock any product at any time from the Edit modal.

  2. 2
    Add your team

    Go to Manage → Team. Your unique invite code is shown at the top. Send it to your breakers and sorters — they go to the login page, click "Team member? Join with invite code", and create their account. They appear in your Team list automatically with the role you set.

  3. 3
    Set each employee's pay structure

    In Manage → Team, click Pay Settings next to each employee. Choose their pay type (commission, hourly, salary, or a combination) and enter their rate. Breakers and sorters have different pay type options — see the Team & Pay Settings section below for a full breakdown.

  4. 4
    Create your first schedule

    Go to Live → Schedule. Assign shifts to your breakers and sorters with a date, start time, stream key, and optional notes. Your team sees their upcoming shifts immediately when they log in.

  5. 5
    Go live

    Your breakers clock in from their dashboard, select products, and submit breaks in real time. You see everything update live in Reports → Dashboard.

  6. 6
    Run payroll at the end of each period

    Go to Manage → Payroll. Select a period (this week, last two weeks, this month, etc.), click Run Payroll, review the breakdown for each employee, then click Mark as Paid when you've paid them out. The run is saved to history automatically.

Tip: The platform tracks hours, commission, per-break counts, and tips automatically once your team is using it. The more consistently your team clocks in and out, the more accurate your payroll numbers will be.

Break Entry — Live Stream Tracking

This is the main screen your breakers use during a live stream. As the owner you can also submit breaks yourself from this tab.

  1. 1
    Enter a Stream Key

    This is the platform event ID or your own label (e.g. "Saturday Night Break"). The system auto-loads any in-progress stream for that key so breakers can pick up where it left off.

  2. 2
    Select products

    Use the quantity fields next to each product. Box cost and break even update live. Filter by category using the pill buttons above the product table.

  3. 3
    Enter spots and fee %

    Spots = how many slots you sold. Platform Fee % = your platform fee (typically 8%). The Break Even banner shows you the minimum you need to earn before you go live — this is an estimate based on the fee % you entered.

  4. 4
    Submit breaks as you go

    After each break, type the current running platform total and click SUBMIT BREAK. The system calculates each individual break's revenue automatically (new total minus previous total). Stock decrements and an inventory log entry is created for each break submitted.

  5. 5
    Close Out Stream

    At the end of the stream, enter the Final platform Sales number (what platform actually pays out after their cut) and click CLOSE OUT STREAM. The real platform fee is calculated as the difference between your total submitted revenue and the final payout. This locks in all numbers and moves the stream to History.

Tip: The "Current platform Total" field is cumulative — type the running total shown on platform screen, not each individual break's amount. The app subtracts for you.
Fee note: The fee % on the break calculator is an estimate used for break-even planning. The real fee is only known at close-out, when you enter your Final platform Sales number. Net profit on the Dashboard reflects the real fee automatically.

Inventory

  1. 1
    Add a product

    Name, cost per box, category, and starting stock quantity. Category is used for filtering during breaks and grouping in Product ROI reports. If you check "Track Average Cost", the system will maintain a weighted average cost as you restock.

  2. 2
    Stock tracks automatically

    Each time a break is submitted, the quantity used is deducted from the product's stock. You don't need to update this manually after breaks.

  3. 3
    Restock a product

    Click Edit on any product, then go to the Restock section. Enter how many boxes you're adding and the price you paid per box. The system automatically recalculates your weighted average cost — so your box cost on future breaks reflects the blended price across all your purchases, not just the newest batch.

  4. 4
    Weighted average cost explained

    Formula: (existing stock × current cost + restock qty × restock cost) ÷ (existing + restock). Example: you have 10 boxes at $80 and restock 5 more at $95 → new avg cost = $85. Your break-even calculations always use the most accurate cost.

  5. 5
    Edit and delete

    Click Edit to update name, cost, category, or stock. Deleting a product removes it from future breaks but does not affect past history or inventory log entries.

Tip: Every restock is automatically recorded in the Inventory Log, giving you a full purchase history per product.

Inventory Log

The Inventory Log is a full transaction history of every box that has entered or left your inventory. Find it under Manage → Inventory Log.

  1. 1
    What gets logged automatically

    Initial When you first add a product with stock. Restock Every time you restock from the Edit modal. Used Every break submitted during a live stream.

  2. 2
    Filter by period, action, or product

    Use the period dropdown, action filter (All / Restock / Used), and product dropdown to narrow down exactly what you want to see. Filters apply instantly.

  3. 3
    Summary strip

    At the top of the log you'll see total units in (restocks), total units out (used in breaks), and the net change for the selected period. This makes it easy to reconcile your physical inventory against the system.

Use case: If your physical count doesn't match the system, filter the log by product and scan for any breaks that used the wrong quantity, or restocks that weren't entered.

Reports & Analytics

📈 Dashboard

Total revenue, net profit, platform fees, and units sold across all closed streams for the selected period. Net profit reflects real fees — not estimates.

📋 Stream Summary

Full breakdown of a single stream — every break, revenue, costs, real fees, and final profit for that session.

🗂 Break History

Every individual break logged, filterable by date. See which breaks made money and which didn't.

🏷 Product ROI

Which boxes make you the most money? See revenue, cost, and profit per product across all time. Uses weighted average cost for accuracy.

🏆 Performance

Switch between Breaker and Sorter views. Breakers are ranked by revenue, profit, and margins. Sorters are ranked by tasks completed, streams sorted, and hours worked.

Note: Payroll is not automatically deducted from Dashboard profit — it is tracked separately in the Payroll tab. This keeps your revenue reporting clean and lets you see profit before and after labor costs.

Team & Pay Settings

  1. 1
    Get the invite code

    Your unique invite code is displayed at the top of the Team tab. Share it with anyone you want to add — breakers and sorters both use the same code. When they register, they select their role.

  2. 2
    Set each person's pay structure

    Click Pay Settings next to any employee. Choose their pay type from the list. You can change this at any time — it only affects future payroll calculations.

  3. 3
    Employees see their own pay breakdown

    Breakers and sorters both have a My Pay tab in their dashboard where they can see their hours worked, breaks sorted, commissions earned, and tips. They see exactly what the payroll system shows — no surprises.

Breaker Pay Types

Commission Only

Paid a % of net profit from streams they worked. Net profit = Final Sales − Box Cost − Other Costs.

Hourly Only

Paid a flat hourly rate based on clocked shift hours. No commission.

Salary Only

Fixed weekly or monthly amount regardless of hours or streams.

Commission + Hourly

Earns both a commission % on net profit and an hourly rate for their clocked hours.

Commission + Salary

Fixed salary amount plus commission % on top of that for streams worked.

Sorter Pay Types

Hourly Only

Paid a flat hourly rate based on clocked shift hours.

Per-Break Only

Paid a fixed dollar amount for each sort task completed.

Hourly + Per-Break

Earns both an hourly rate for time clocked and a per-sort rate for each task completed.

Note: Pay rates are set by you and are never visible to other employees — only to the individual employee they apply to. Payouts are tracked and calculated in the system but paid manually by you. Card Break Pro does not transfer funds.

Payroll — Run & Record

The Payroll tab lets you calculate exactly what you owe each employee for any period, review the full breakdown, and mark it as paid. Find it under Manage → Payroll.

  1. 1
    Select a period

    Choose from preset periods — This Week, Last Two Weeks, This Month, Last Month — or set a custom date range. The payroll engine pulls all shift hours, closed streams, and sort tasks from that window.

  2. 2
    Filter by role (optional)

    Use the role filter to view All Staff, Breakers Only, or Sorters Only. Useful when you pay breakers and sorters on different schedules.

  3. 3
    Click Run Payroll

    The system calculates pay for every employee based on their individual pay type:

    • Commission employees → net profit × their commission rate

    • Hourly employees → hours clocked × hourly rate

    • Salary employees → prorated salary for the period

    • Per-break sorters → sort tasks completed × per-break rate

    • Combination types → both components added together

    Tips are always added to the total owed for each breaker, regardless of pay type. Since tips on platform go into the seller's account, the breaker must report how much they received — and the payroll run reflects what you owe them back.

  4. 4
    Review the breakdown

    The results table shows each employee's hours, streams worked, breaks sorted, commission, base pay, tips, and total owed. The summary strip at the top shows total payroll, breaker total, sorter total, and your net profit after payroll.

  5. 5
    Data quality warnings

    If any employee has no pay type configured, has unclosed shifts (clocked in but never out), or is on hourly pay but shows 0 hours — an amber warning appears with their names so you can fix it before running numbers.

  6. 6
    Mark as Paid

    Once you've paid your team, click Mark as Paid. You can add an optional note (e.g. "Paid via Venmo"). The run is saved to Payroll History with the date, period, total paid, and staff count. You can never accidentally overwrite a past run.

  7. 7
    View past runs

    The Payroll History table at the bottom of the tab shows all completed payroll runs. Click into any run to see the full breakdown exactly as it was when you marked it paid.

Best practice: Always run payroll after streams are closed out — commission is calculated from closed stream net profit. Open streams are excluded from payroll calculations.
Accuracy tip: Payroll is only as good as the data going into it. Make sure your team is clocking in and out consistently, sort tasks are being logged, and pay types are configured before you run.

Schedule

  1. 1
    Create a shift

    Select a team member (breaker or sorter), date, start time, stream key, and optional notes. Click Schedule Shift. The employee sees it immediately in their My Shifts tab when they log in.

  2. 2
    Clock in / Clock out

    When a team member starts their shift, they clock in from their dashboard — the shift turns yellow (IN PROGRESS). Breakers are automatically clocked out when they close out a stream. Sorters clock out manually when their sort is done.

  3. 3
    Hours feed directly into payroll

    Clock-in and clock-out times are the source of truth for all hourly pay calculations. No manual hour entry needed — the schedule system handles it.

  4. 4
    Edit or cancel shifts

    Use the Edit and Cancel buttons in the upcoming shifts table. Cancelled shifts remain visible but are marked cancelled — they are excluded from payroll hours.

  5. 5
    Calendar view

    The monthly calendar shows color-coded shift chips. Navigate months with the arrows. Status colors: blue = scheduled, yellow = in progress, green = completed.

Manager Role

  1. 1
    What managers can do

    Managers get their own dashboard with access to: Schedule (create and manage shifts for all roles), Inventory (add products, restock), Inventory Log, Sort Queue, and full Reports — including revenue, break history, product ROI, and the Performance tab.

  2. 2
    What managers cannot do

    Managers do not submit breaks, cannot access Payroll, cannot view or change pay rates, and have no access to billing or the Reset Data option. Those remain owner-only.

  3. 3
    Invite a manager

    Go to Team → Generate Invite Code and share the code with your manager. On the Join page, they select the Manager role card when signing up. They are immediately linked to your business and redirected to their manager dashboard.

  4. 4
    Set manager pay

    Managers appear in your Team list with a purple badge. Click Pay Settings next to their name to set their pay type — typically Hourly or Salary. Their pay is tracked in Payroll just like any other team member.

  5. 5
    Performance tab — Breakers vs Sorters

    Both you and your managers can see the Performance tab under Reports. Use the toggle at the top to switch between Breaker performance (revenue, profit, margin, avg per break) and Sorter performance (tasks completed, streams sorted, hours worked). Useful for reviews, scheduling decisions, and pay conversations.

Settings

  1. 1
    Profile

    Update your display name and phone number. Your name appears on schedules and in team views.

  2. 2
    Change Email

    Update the email address on your account. A confirmation link is sent to both your old and new address before the change takes effect.

  3. 3
    Change Password

    Enter a new password to update your login credentials. You'll stay logged in after the change.

  4. 4
    Time Format

    Switch between 12-hour (12:00 PM) and 24-hour (14:00) display across all schedule views.

  5. 5
    Billing

    Manage your Card Break Pro subscription from the Billing tab — view your plan, update payment method, or cancel. Powered by Stripe.

  6. 6
    Reset Data

    Permanently deletes all breaks, streams, payroll runs, inventory log entries, and break history for your organization. Use only if you need to start completely fresh.

Warning: Reset Data is irreversible. Your inventory products, team accounts, schedules, and org settings are not deleted — only break and stream history, payroll runs, and inventory log entries.
